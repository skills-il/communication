# Israeli Business Calendar

Key dates, deadlines, and seasonal patterns for running a business or freelance practice in Israel. Use this as a planning reference when scheduling projects, setting deadlines, or preparing for slow periods.

---

## Recurring Monthly Deadlines

These deadlines repeat every month or every two months throughout the year.

| Date | Obligation | Who It Applies To |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 15th of each month | VAT (מע"מ) payment and reporting | Businesses on monthly VAT cycle (turnover above threshold) |
| 15th of each month | Bituach Leumi (ביטוח לאומי) advance payment | Self-employed (עצמאים) |
| 15th of each month | Income tax advance (מקדמת מס הכנסה) | Self-employed with advance payment obligation |
| 15th of every odd month | VAT reporting - bi-monthly cycle | Businesses on bi-monthly VAT cycle |

**VAT reporting periods (bi-monthly):**

| Reporting Period | Report Due By |
|-----------------|---------------|
| January-February | March 15 |
| March-April | May 15 |
| May-June | July 15 |
| July-August | September 15 |
| September-October | November 15 |
| November-December | January 15 (following year) |

Note: When the 15th falls on Shabbat or a holiday, the deadline shifts to the next business day. Always verify with the Tax Authority (www.gov.il/taxes).

---

## Annual Tax Deadlines

These change slightly each year. Treat these as approximate; confirm current year dates with your accountant.

| Obligation | Approximate Period | Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| Annual income tax return (דוח שנתי) - self-employed | April-May | With accountant extension: often pushed to June-July |
| Annual income tax return - employees with additional income | April-May | - |
| Employer annual reports (102 forms) | February | Report on employee wages from previous year |
| Pension contributions reconciliation | Quarterly | Varies by pension fund |

## Jewish Holiday Calendar (5786 / 2025-2026)

Note: Hebrew calendar dates shift each Gregorian year. The dates below are for the 5786 cycle, verified against HebCal (https://www.hebcal.com). Always re-verify before relying on specific dates.

### High Holidays / Yamim Noraim (ימים נוראים)

| Holiday | 2025 Gregorian Date | Business Impact |
|---------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Erev Rosh Hashana | Sep 22, 2025 | Short day (closes early) |
| Rosh Hashana (ראש השנה) | Sep 23-24, 2025 | Full shutdown 2 days. Many take Sep 22-26 off |
| Erev Yom Kippur | Oct 1, 2025 | Short day (closes early) |
| Yom Kippur (יום כיפור) | Oct 2, 2025 | Complete national shutdown. Even secular Israelis don't work |
| Erev Sukkot | Oct 6, 2025 | Short day (closes early) |
| Sukkot - first days (סוכות) | Oct 7-8, 2025 | Full shutdown |
| Sukkot - Chol HaMoed | Oct 9-13, 2025 | Partial operation; many on vacation. Oct 13 is Hoshana Raba |
| Shmini Atzeret / Simchat Torah | Oct 14-15, 2025 | Full shutdown |

**Planning note:** The Tishrei holiday cluster (Rosh Hashana through Simchat Torah) effectively shuts down Israeli business for most of September 22 through October 15. Build this into any project timeline that spans September-November.

## Seasonal Business Patterns

### Q1 (January-March)

**Pace:** Moderate to slow in January, picks up in February-March.

- January: Post-holiday slow start. Many decisions deferred from previous year
- February: Budget approvals typically land; new annual contracts kick in
- March: Purim disrupts one week. Fiscal year-end planning for companies on April-March cycle
- **VAT reminder:** Jan-Feb report due March 15

### Q2 (April-June)

**Pace:** Very disrupted. Pesach dominates April.

- April: Pesach effectively halves the month. Chol HaMoed productivity is low
- May: Yom HaZikaron/Yom HaAtzmaut back-to-back creates a mini-holiday period
- June: Strong productivity month. Good for closing deals and launching projects
- **Annual tax return season.** Self-employed freelancers and businesses submit annual reports
- **VAT reminder:** Mar-Apr report due May 15; May-Jun due July 15

### Q3 (July-September)

**Pace:** Slow in July-August, then urgent before holidays.

- July-August: Israeli vacation season. Response rates drop significantly
- August: Many businesses at 50-70% capacity. Not a good month to launch anything important
- September: Back-to-school (usually first week of September). Then High Holiday rush begins
- **September alert:** Rosh Hashana typically falls in September. Everything before the holidays becomes urgent; plan accordingly
- **VAT reminder:** Jul-Aug report due Sep 15

### Q4 (October-December)

**Pace:** Very disrupted in October, strong in November-December.

- October: Tishrei holidays dominate. Most of October is effectively lost for major work
- November: Strong productivity month after holidays. Good for year-end pushes
- December: Chanukah falls here but does not close businesses. December 31 has no special meaning in Israel (fiscal year-end varies by company)
- **VAT reminder:** Sep-Oct report due Nov 15; Nov-Dec report due Jan 15

## Key Planning Rules

**For deadlines:** Add 1-2 weeks buffer for anything due in October (Tishrei holidays), the week of Pesach, or August.

**For launches:** Avoid launching products, campaigns, or major projects in the two weeks before Rosh Hashana, the week of Pesach, or August. The audience is distracted or absent.

**For government filings:** File early. The Tax Authority and Bituach Leumi websites often slow down near deadlines.

**For client proposals:** Avoid sending proposals right before major holidays. They will get lost. Send right after holidays when people are catching up.

**For hiring:** Q1 (Jan-Feb) and post-Pesach (late April, May) are the best hiring windows. Avoid making offers right before the Tishrei holidays; decisions get delayed until after.
