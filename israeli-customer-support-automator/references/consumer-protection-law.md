# Israeli Consumer Protection Law 1981 (חוק הגנת הצרכן, תשמ"א-1981)

## Overview

The Consumer Protection Law (chok haganat ha'tzarchan) is Israel's primary consumer rights statute. It governs the relationship between businesses (oskim) and consumers (tzarchanim) and establishes protections for purchases, advertising, pricing, returns, and complaints.

## Key Provisions

### Section 2: Misleading Actions (hata'a)

A business shall not mislead a consumer regarding any material aspect of a transaction, including:

| Misleading Action | Description |
|---|---|
| Nature of goods/services | Misrepresenting what is being sold |
| Quality or quantity | Overstating quality, hiding defects |
| Ingredients or composition | Incorrect labeling or description |
| Manufacturing date or origin | False claims about when/where made |
| Price | Hidden fees, bait-and-switch pricing |
| Terms of transaction | Undisclosed conditions, restrictions |
| Warranty or service | Overstating warranty coverage |
| Endorsements | Fake reviews, fabricated testimonials |

**Liability:** A business that misleads a consumer is liable for any damage caused, including compensatory and punitive damages.

### Section 10: Warranty (achrayut)

| Warranty Type | Description | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Written warranty | Must honor all terms stated in writing | As specified in warranty document |
| Implied warranty | Products must be fit for their intended purpose | Reasonable period based on product type |
| Manufacturing defect | Right to repair, replacement, or refund | No specific time limit |

**Business obligations:**
- Must provide warranty terms in Hebrew before purchase
- Cannot disclaim implied warranties for consumer goods
- Must honor warranty regardless of where the product was purchased (within Israel)
- Warranty service must be available within a reasonable geographic area

### Section 14A: Door-to-Door Sales (iskai rechov)

| Provision | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Written contract | Must provide a signed written contract |
| Cooling-off period | Consumer can cancel within 14 days from contract or receipt of goods |
| Cancellation method | Written notice to the business |
| Refund | Full refund within 14 days of cancellation |
| Exceptions | Limited (fewer than remote purchases) |

### Section 14G: Remote Purchases (iskat mechira merachok)

This is the most frequently referenced section in customer support operations.

**Definition:** A "remote purchase" (iskat rechisha merachok) includes any purchase made without the consumer and business being physically present together, such as online purchases, phone orders, catalog orders, and TV shopping.

**Cooling-off period (tekufat tzinun):**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 14 days |
| Starts from | The later of: (1) delivery date, or (2) date consumer received contract terms and cancellation details |
| Cancellation method | Any reasonable method (email, phone, letter, in person) |
| Business response | Must acknowledge cancellation and process refund within 14 days |
| Cancellation fee | Maximum of 5% of transaction price or NIS 100, whichever is lower |

**Extended cooling-off periods:**

| Consumer Category | Cooling-off Period |
|---|---|
| Standard consumer | 14 days |
| New immigrant (oleh chadash, within 2 years) | 4 months |
| Person with disability | 4 months |
| Senior citizen (65+) | 4 months |

**Exceptions to cooling-off period (consumer CANNOT cancel):**

| Exception | Reason |
|---|---|
| Perishable goods | Cannot be resold after return |
| Custom-made or altered products | Made specifically for the consumer |
| Opened information products | Software, recordings, digital content once unsealed |
| Accommodation/travel/entertainment for specific date | Time-bound services |
| Newspapers and magazines | Subscription-based periodicals |

**Refund rules:**

| Scenario | Refund Amount | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Cancellation within cooling-off (product returned) | Full price minus cancellation fee (max 5% or NIS 100) | Within 14 days of receiving cancellation notice |
| Cancellation within cooling-off (service not yet provided) | Full price minus cancellation fee | Within 14 days |
| Defective product (any time) | Full refund, or repair, or replacement (consumer's choice) | Reasonable timeframe |
| Product not as described | Full refund | Reasonable timeframe |

### Section 14B: Receipt Requirements

| Transaction Amount | Receipt Required? | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Over NIS 24 | Yes (mandatory) | Must include: business name, date, item description, price, VAT amount |
| Under NIS 24 | Upon consumer request | Same details as above |

### Section 17A: Price Display

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Displayed prices | Must include VAT (ma'am) |
| Currency | Must be in Israeli New Shekel (NIS) |
| Binding price | The displayed price is binding; business cannot charge more |
| Price errors | Business is generally bound by displayed price (court discretion for obvious errors) |

## Enforcement

### Consumer Protection Authority (Rashut leHaganat haTzarchan)

| Function | Details |
|---|---|
| Website | gov.il (search: Rashut Haganat HaTzarchan) |
| Filing complaints | Online portal, by phone, or in writing |
| Investigation | Can investigate businesses, issue fines, and initiate criminal proceedings |
| Penalties | Administrative fines up to NIS 226,000 per violation (2025 rates) |

### Small Claims Court (Beit Mishpat leTvi'ot Ktanot)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum claim amount | NIS 38,900 (updated annually) |
| Legal representation | Not permitted (consumer and business represent themselves) |
| Filing fee | Approximately NIS 1% of claim amount |
| Timeline | Typically 2-6 months from filing to hearing |

### Civil Courts

For claims exceeding small claims court limits, consumers can file in Magistrate Court (Beit Mishpat Shalom) for claims up to NIS 2.5 million, or District Court (Beit Mishpat Mechozi) for larger amounts.

## Practical Guidelines for Customer Support Teams

### When a Customer Requests a Return

1. **Determine purchase type:** In-store vs remote purchase (different rules apply)
2. **Check timing:** Is the request within the cooling-off period?
3. **Check exceptions:** Does the product fall under any exception category?
4. **Process immediately:** If eligible, process the return and issue refund
5. **Calculate fees:** Cancellation fee is max 5% of price or NIS 100, whichever is lower
6. **Document everything:** Keep records of the return request, communication, and refund

### When a Customer Files a Complaint

1. **Acknowledge within SLA:** Send written acknowledgment of the complaint
2. **Investigate:** Review the transaction, product, and customer history
3. **Respond:** Provide a substantive response within a reasonable timeframe
4. **Offer resolution:** Repair, replacement, refund, or compensation as appropriate
5. **Escalate if needed:** If the customer mentions legal action or the Consumer Protection Authority, escalate to management immediately
6. **Document:** Maintain complete records of the complaint and resolution process

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Legal Risk |
|---|---|
| Refusing returns within 14-day cooling-off period | Direct violation of Section 14G; administrative fine + civil liability |
| Charging more than the maximum cancellation fee | Violation of Section 14G; refund of excess amount required |
| Not honoring displayed price | Violation of Section 17A; must sell at displayed price |
| Making customers pay return shipping for defective products | Business bears shipping cost for defective products |
| Requiring original packaging for defective product returns | No legal basis; product can be returned in any reasonable condition |
| Ignoring complaints from consumers with extended cooling-off rights | Extended periods for seniors, new immigrants, and people with disabilities must be honored |
