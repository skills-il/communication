# Israeli Job Platforms: Detailed Guide

A comprehensive guide to finding tech jobs across Israeli platforms. Each section covers platform strengths, optimization tips, and search strategies.

## Overview of the Israeli Tech Job Market

The Israeli tech job market has several unique characteristics:

- **Geography**: Most tech jobs are concentrated in the "Silicon Wadi" corridor: Tel Aviv, Herzliya, Ra'anana, Petah Tikva, and Kfar Saba. Haifa and Be'er Sheva have growing tech scenes. Jerusalem has a smaller but active market
- **Work week**: Sunday to Thursday (Friday is a half-day or off, Saturday is Shabbat)
- **Hiring seasons**: Active hiring January-March and September-November. Slower during summer (July-August) and major holiday periods (September/October Jewish holidays, Passover in April)
- **Market size**: Approximately 400,000 tech workers in Israel (2025 data)
- **Languages**: Most tech work is conducted in English, but daily office communication is often in Hebrew. International companies tend to be English-first

## Platform Details

### AllJobs (alljobs.co.il)

**Overview:**
AllJobs is Israel's largest and longest-running job board, founded in 2000. It covers all industries but has significant tech listings. The platform is Hebrew-first with some English support.

**Registration:**
1. Create an account at alljobs.co.il
2. Complete your profile in Hebrew
3. Upload your CV (PDF recommended)
4. Set job preferences (technology, location, salary, full/part-time)

**Profile Tips:**
- Complete all profile sections (completeness affects visibility)
- Use Hebrew for your profile summary
- List technologies in both Hebrew and English
- Set a realistic salary range (this is shared with employers)
- Upload separate Hebrew and English CVs if available
- Enable "display CV to employers" for passive search

**Search Strategy:**
- Primary categories: "הייטק ומחשבים" (Hi-Tech and Computers), "תוכנה" (Software)
- Sub-categories available: Frontend, Backend, Full Stack, DevOps, QA, Data, Security, etc.
- Location filter: Major tech hubs (Tel Aviv, Herzliya, Ra'anana, Petah Tikva, Haifa)
- Salary filter: Set minimum to 15,000 NIS to filter non-tech roles
- Job type: "משרה מלאה" (Full-time), "עבודה מהבית" (Work from home)

**Job Alert Setup:**
- Create multiple alerts for different search criteria
- Combine technology keywords with location
- Set alert frequency to daily for active job search
- Use both Hebrew ("פיתוח") and English ("development") keywords

**Pros:**
- Largest database of Israeli job listings
- Good for finding positions at traditional Israeli companies
- Salary data available
- Resume database visible to employers

**Cons:**
- Interface can feel dated
- Many recruiter postings (not direct employers)
- Less popular with international startups
- Some listings may be outdated

### Drushim (drushim.co.il)

**Overview:**
Drushim (meaning "Wanted" in Hebrew) is a popular job aggregator that pulls listings from company career pages, LinkedIn, and other sources. It has evolved into a comprehensive job platform.

**Registration:**
1. Sign up at drushim.co.il
2. Create a tech-focused profile
3. Upload your CV
4. Set technology preferences

**Profile Tips:**
- Select specific technologies from their predefined list
- Add frameworks and tools (not just languages)
- Set experience level accurately
- Follow companies you are interested in
- Enable push notifications via the mobile app

**Search Strategy:**
- Use the "הייטק" category for tech-specific results
- Filter by technology stack (dropdown menus)
- Filter by experience: ג'וניור (Junior), מידלבל (Mid), סניור (Senior), ליד (Lead)
- Check "חברות מגייסות" for companies actively hiring
- Use the salary filter to narrow results
- Sort by "relevance" rather than "date" for better matches

**Company Pages:**
- Drushim has company profiles with employee reviews
- Check company culture ratings before applying
- Look at "benefits" section for compensation insights
- See how many open positions a company has (indicator of growth)

**Pros:**
- Aggregates from multiple sources
- Good technology-specific filtering
- Company reviews and culture info
- Active mobile app with push notifications

**Cons:**
- Some listings are aggregated and may link to external sites
- Review data may be limited for smaller companies
- Fewer international company listings

### LinkedIn Israel

**Overview:**
LinkedIn is the dominant professional networking platform in Israeli tech. Most recruiters, HR managers, and engineering leaders actively use it. It is the single most important platform for Israeli tech job search.

**Profile Optimization (Critical):**

1. **Headline**: Write in English. Include your role, key technologies, and a differentiator.
   ```
   Good: "Senior Backend Engineer | Node.js & Python | Building scalable systems at [Company]"
   Bad: "Seeking new opportunities"
   ```

2. **Summary/About**: Write a compelling 3-5 sentence summary. Include:
   - What you do and what you are passionate about
   - Key technologies and domains
   - Notable achievements or experience
   - What you are looking for (optional, only if actively searching)
   - Consider adding a Hebrew paragraph for local recruiters

3. **Experience**: Detail each role with:
   - Clear title (use English titles)
   - Company name and location
   - 3-5 bullet points with achievements (quantified when possible)
   - Technologies used

4. **Skills and Endorsements**:
   - List your top 50 skills
   - Arrange them with most relevant first
   - Request endorsements from colleagues
   - Skills with endorsements rank higher in recruiter searches

5. **Recommendations**: Get 3-5 recommendations from managers, colleagues, or reports

6. **Additional sections**:
   - Certifications (AWS, GCP, etc.)
   - Publications or patents
   - Volunteer experience
   - Languages (list Hebrew and English proficiency)

**Search Strategy:**
- Use "Israel" location filter
- Search by company name if targeting specific companies
- Use boolean operators: "Backend AND (Node.js OR Python) AND Israel"
- Set job alerts for automated daily notifications
- Filter by "Easy Apply" for quick applications
- Check "LinkedIn Jobs" tab regularly

**Networking Strategy:**
- Connect with Israeli tech leaders and recruiters
- Join groups: "Israeli High-Tech", "Startup Nation", "Tel Aviv Tech Community"
- Engage with posts (comment, share) to increase visibility
- Share your own content about technology topics
- Attend LinkedIn-promoted Israeli tech events

**Recruiter Interaction:**
- Israeli tech recruiters are very active on LinkedIn
- Respond to InMails even if not interested (maintain relationships)
- Be clear about your preferences (role, salary, location, WFH policy)
- Ask about the company, team, and technical challenges
- It is acceptable to ask about salary range early in the conversation

**Pros:**
- Essential for Israeli tech networking
- Largest recruiter activity
- Best for international companies in Israel
- Strong content and community features

**Cons:**
- Many unsolicited recruiter messages (can be overwhelming)
- Not all Israeli companies post here (some prefer Hebrew platforms)
- Premium features require paid subscription

### JobMaster (jobmaster.co.il)

**Overview:**
JobMaster is an Israeli job platform with a clean interface and good tech coverage. It focuses on direct employer listings rather than recruiter postings.

**Registration:**
1. Create account at jobmaster.co.il
2. Build your profile in Hebrew
3. Upload CV
4. Set preferences

**Search Strategy:**
- Filter by "הייטק" category
- Use location filter for central Israel tech hubs
- Check salary ranges (many listings include them)
- Set up email alerts for matching positions

**Pros:**
- Clean, modern interface
- More direct employer listings
- Salary transparency
- Less recruiter noise

**Cons:**
- Smaller database than AllJobs
- Less known among international companies
- Fewer advanced filtering options

### Company Career Pages and ATS Platforms

Many Israeli tech companies post jobs on their own career pages using ATS platforms:

**Comeet (comeet.com):**
- Used by many Israeli startups and scale-ups
- Examples: Wiz, Papaya Global, Lightricks
- Tip: Apply directly through the company's Comeet page rather than through aggregators
- Mentioning an employee referral significantly increases response rate

**Lever (lever.co):**
- Used by mid-size Israeli tech companies
- Examples: Monday.com, Gong, Snyk
- Tip: A tailored cover letter helps stand out on Lever

**Greenhouse (greenhouse.io):**
- Used by larger Israeli tech companies
- Examples: Wix, ironSource, Fiverr
- Tip: Follow company career pages for early access to new positions

**Workday (workday.com):**
- Used by enterprise companies with Israel offices
- Examples: Intel, IBM, Oracle
- Tip: These have more formal application processes with structured forms

**BreezyHR:**
- Used by smaller Israeli startups
- Tip: Applications are often reviewed by founders directly

### Government and Public Sector

**Gov.il Jobs:**
- Government tech positions posted at gov.il
- Typically lower salaries but better job security and benefits
- Often require Israeli citizenship
- Good for those interested in civic tech and digital government

**IDF and Defense Industry:**
- Defense companies (Rafael, Elbit, IAI) post on their career pages
- Security clearance often required
- Strong benefits packages
- Technology often cutting-edge (cyber, autonomous systems, radar)

## Job Search Timeline

### Active Job Search (Target: 4-8 weeks)

**Week 1: Preparation**
- Update LinkedIn profile (English)
- Prepare Hebrew and English CVs
- Research target companies and roles
- Set salary expectations using benchmark data
- Register on AllJobs, Drushim, JobMaster

**Weeks 2-3: Application Phase**
- Apply to 10-15 positions per week
- Prioritize direct applications over recruiter postings
- Send personalized messages to hiring managers on LinkedIn
- Start networking (reach out to 5-10 contacts per week)

**Weeks 4-5: Interview Phase**
- Prepare for phone screens (review company, prepare questions)
- Practice technical interview skills
- Prepare system design discussions (for senior roles)
- Schedule interviews strategically (not all in one day)

**Weeks 6-8: Decision Phase**
- Compare offers using total compensation (not just salary)
- Negotiate strategically (see negotiation section in SKILL.md)
- Check references on the company (talk to current/former employees)
- Make decision and sign

### Passive Job Search (Ongoing)

- Keep LinkedIn profile updated and active
- Engage with tech community content weekly
- Maintain relationships with 2-3 trusted recruiters
- Set job alerts on all platforms
- Attend 1-2 tech meetups per month
- Update CV every 6 months

## Tips for Maximum Coverage

1. **Register on all four platforms** to maximize visibility
2. **LinkedIn is non-negotiable** for Israeli tech job search
3. **Set up alerts on all platforms** with different keyword combinations
4. **Apply directly on company career pages** when possible (higher response rate)
5. **Keep all profiles synchronized** with the same information
6. **Track applications centrally** using the tracking template in SKILL.md
7. **Respond to all recruiter messages** even with "not interested" (the market is small)
8. **Join Israeli tech communities** on Facebook, Telegram, and Discord for unlisted positions
