# Mandatory Employment Benefits in Israel 2026

## Pension (pensia)

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Employee contribution | 6.0% of gross salary | Mandatory Pension Order (Tzav Harchavat Pensia) |
| Employer contribution | 6.5% of gross salary | Same order |
| Employer severance component | Included in 6.5% (or separate 8.33%) | Same order |
| Effective from | Day 1 for employees with existing pension; after 6 months for new employees without pension | Extension Order 2008, amended 2017 |
| Applies to | All employees age 21+ (men) or 20+ (women) | Same order |
| Salary ceiling for contributions | Full salary unless employment agreement caps it | Common practice vs legal minimum |

**Notes:**
- The employer's 6.5% typically includes 6% toward the pension fund and 0.5% toward disability insurance (oved bi'tuach ovedim)
- Many tech companies offer above-minimum contributions (e.g., 7% + 7.5%)

## Keren Hishtalmut (קרן השתלמות)

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Employee contribution | 2.5% of gross salary | Standard rate |
| Employer contribution | 7.5% of gross salary | Standard rate |
| Mandatory? | No | Not legally required, but considered standard in most sectors |
| Tax benefit (employee) | Contributions tax-exempt up to salary ceiling | Ceiling: approximately NIS 15,712/month (2025), updated annually |
| Tax benefit (employer) | Contributions tax-exempt up to same ceiling | Same ceiling |
| Withdrawal period | After 6 years (tax-free) or 3 years (taxable) | 6-year lock-in for tax exemption |
| Common in | Tech, finance, professional services | Almost universal in Israeli tech sector |

## Severance Pay (pitzuyei piturim)

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rate | 8.33% of last monthly salary per year of service | Equivalent to one month's salary per year |
| Trigger | Dismissal, resignation under certain conditions, death | Not automatic on voluntary resignation |
| Section 14 waiver | Pension contributions count toward severance obligation | Must be explicitly stated in employment agreement |
| Statute of limitations | 7 years from dismissal date | |

**Section 14 explained:**
- Without Section 14: employer owes full severance (last salary x years) on top of pension contributions
- With Section 14: pension contributions replace severance obligation; employee keeps pension fund balance; employer has no additional liability
- Most Israeli employers use Section 14 as standard practice
- Requires a signed general approval letter (ishur klali le-fi siman 14)

## Vacation Days (yemei chufsha)

| Years of Service | Calendar Days | Workdays (5-day week) | Workdays (6-day week) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-4 | 14 | 10 | 12 |
| 5 | 16 | 11 | 13 |
| 6 | 18 | 13 | 15 |
| 7 | 21 | 15 | 17 |
| 8 | 22 | 16 | 18 |
| 9 | 23 | 16 | 19 |
| 10 | 24 | 17 | 20 |
| 11 | 25 | 18 | 21 |
| 12 | 26 | 18 | 22 |
| 13 | 27 | 19 | 23 |
| 14+ | 28 | 20 | 24 |

**Notes:**
- "Calendar days" include Saturdays but not official rest days
- Minimum is per the Annual Leave Law 1951 (chok chufsha shnati)
- Many tech companies offer 20-25 workdays from day 1, exceeding the legal minimum
- Unused vacation days can be accumulated for up to 2 years (beyond that, employer can require usage)

## Sick Days (yemei machala)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Accrual rate | 1.5 days per month |
| Maximum accumulation | 90 days |
| First day | Unpaid |
| Days 2-3 | 50% pay |
| Day 4 onward | 100% pay |
| Medical certificate | Required from first day (employer may waive for 1-2 days) |
| Bereavement leave (yemei avel) | 7 days (deducted from sick days) for first-degree relatives |

## Convalescence Pay (dmei havra'a)

| Years of Service | Days per Year | Approximate Annual Amount (2025 rates) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 5 | NIS 2,090 |
| 2-3 | 6 | NIS 2,508 |
| 4-10 | 7 | NIS 2,926 |
| 11-15 | 8 | NIS 3,344 |
| 16-19 | 9 | NIS 3,762 |
| 20+ | 10 | NIS 4,180 |

**Notes:**
- Daily rate: NIS 418 per day (as of 2025; updated annually by extension order)
- Typically paid as lump sum in June-September
- Paid to all employees after completing their first year
- Pro-rated for part-time employees based on scope of position (heikef misra)
- Per the extension order applying General Collective Agreement provisions

## Overtime Pay (sha'ot nosafot)

| Overtime Hours | Pay Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| First 2 hours (per day) | 125% of regular hourly rate | Applies after 8.6 hours in a regular day or 7.6 hours on Friday |
| Beyond 2 hours (per day) | 150% of regular hourly rate | |
| Weekly cap | 12 overtime hours per week | Hours of Work and Rest Law |
| Daily cap | Varies; generally 12 total hours including overtime | |
| Shabbat/holiday work | 150% of regular rate | Must also provide compensatory rest day |

**Global overtime ("sha'ot globali'ot"):**
- Common in tech: fixed monthly overtime payment covering a set number of hours (e.g., 20-30 hours)
- Must be specified in the employment agreement
- If actual overtime exceeds the global amount, employer must pay the difference
- Cannot be used to circumvent minimum overtime rates

## Travel Expenses (hoza'ot nesi'a)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Basis | Public transport cost from residence to workplace |
| Daily cap | Updated periodically; approximately NIS 22.60 per day (2025) |
| Monthly cap | Based on actual work days x daily rate |
| Alternative | Employer-provided transportation, parking, or car allowance |
| Remote work | Generally not applicable for fully remote employees |

## Notice Period (hodaa mukdemet)

### For Monthly-Salaried Employees (sachirim)

| Service Duration | Notice Period |
|---|---|
| First 6 months | 1 day per month of employment |
| 7-12 months | 6 days + 2.5 days per month beyond 6 |
| Over 1 year | 1 month |

### For Hourly/Daily Employees

| Service Duration | Notice Period |
|---|---|
| First year | 1 day per month of employment |
| Second year | 14 days + 1 day per 2 months beyond first year |
| Third year | 21 days + 1 day per 2 months beyond second year |
| Over 3 years | 1 month |

**Notes:**
- Notice period applies both ways (employer to employee and employee to employer)
- Employer can pay salary in lieu of notice (pidyon hodaa mukdemet)
- Employee on notice is entitled to 2 hours per day for job searching (for employees with 1+ year of service)
