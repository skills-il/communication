# Equal Employment Opportunities Law 1988 (חוק שוויון הזדמנויות בעבודה, תשמ"ח-1988)

## Overview

The Equal Employment Opportunities Law (chok shivyon hizdamnuyot ba'avoda) is the primary Israeli statute prohibiting employment discrimination. It applies to all stages of employment: recruitment, hiring, working conditions, promotions, training, severance, and benefits.

## Protected Characteristics (Section 2)

The law prohibits discrimination based on the following characteristics:

| # | Protected Characteristic | Hebrew Term | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Gender | מין | Includes gender identity |
| 2 | Sexual orientation | נטייה מינית | Added via amendment |
| 3 | Marital status | מעמד אישי | Single, married, divorced, widowed |
| 4 | Pregnancy | הריון | Includes fertility treatments |
| 5 | Parenthood | הורות | Having or not having children |
| 6 | Age | גיל | No minimum or maximum age restrictions |
| 7 | Race | גזע | |
| 8 | Religion | דת | |
| 9 | Nationality | לאום | |
| 10 | Country of origin | ארץ מוצא | |
| 11 | Ethnicity | עדה | |
| 12 | Place of residence | מקום מגורים | Cannot require specific neighborhood or city |
| 13 | Political views | השקפה פוליטית | |
| 14 | Political party affiliation | שייכות מפלגתית | |
| 15 | Reserve military duty | שירות מילואים | Cannot disadvantage employees due to miluim |
| 16 | Appearance | מראה חיצוני | Height, weight, attractiveness |
| 17 | Disability | מוגבלות | Physical or mental; see also Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law 1998 |

## Scope of Prohibition

The law applies to:

1. **Recruitment and hiring** (kabala la'avoda): Job advertisements, screening criteria, interview questions, hiring decisions
2. **Working conditions** (tna'ei avoda): Salary, benefits, hours, workplace policies
3. **Promotions** (kidumim): Criteria for advancement, training opportunities
4. **Dismissal** (piturim): Termination decisions, selection for layoffs
5. **Retirement** (prisha): Mandatory retirement age policies

## Permitted Exceptions

Discrimination is permitted when:

1. **Genuine occupational qualification (GOQ):** The characteristic is essential to the job function. Examples:
   - Requiring Hebrew fluency for a Hebrew content writing role
   - Gender-specific roles in acting or modeling
   - Security clearance for defense positions

2. **Affirmative action programs:** Preferential treatment to promote representation of underrepresented groups, when authorized by law or collective agreement.

3. **Religious institutions:** Employment at religious institutions where religious observance is a genuine requirement.

4. **Safety requirements:** Physical fitness requirements directly tied to job duties (e.g., firefighters, military positions).

## Burden of Proof

Under Section 9, if an employee establishes a prima facie case of discrimination, the burden of proof shifts to the employer to demonstrate that the action was not discriminatory. This is significant for employers: keeping documentation of hiring decisions and criteria is critical.

## Enforcement and Penalties

| Enforcement Body | Role |
|---|---|
| Israel Equal Employment Opportunities Commission (IEOC) | Investigates complaints, mediates disputes |
| Labor Courts (beit din la'avoda) | Adjudicates employment discrimination claims |
| Attorney General | Can file criminal charges in severe cases |

**Penalties:**
- Compensatory damages (pitzuim): No cap on actual damages
- Punitive damages (pitzuim le'lo hocahat nezek): Up to NIS 120,000 without proof of actual damage (as of 2025 rates, adjusted periodically)
- Criminal penalties: In cases of willful and repeated discrimination
- Injunctive relief: Court orders to cease discriminatory practices

## Practical Compliance Checklist for Job Advertisements

- [ ] No gender-specific language; use inclusive forms ("mefateach/et", "menahel/et")
- [ ] No age range or age-related terms ("young", "energetic" as age proxy)
- [ ] No marital or parental status references ("single preferred", "no children")
- [ ] No pregnancy-related restrictions
- [ ] No military unit or service type requirements (may mention that service is valued but not required)
- [ ] No ethnicity, religion, or national origin preferences
- [ ] No residence requirements unless genuinely job-related (e.g., on-site emergency response)
- [ ] No appearance requirements unless genuinely job-related
- [ ] Clear separation between mandatory requirements (must-have) and preferred qualifications (nice-to-have)
- [ ] Requirements are tied to job functions, not personal characteristics

## Practical Compliance Checklist for Interviews

- [ ] Do not ask about marital status, children, or family plans
- [ ] Do not ask about pregnancy or plans to become pregnant
- [ ] Do not ask about religious observance or practices
- [ ] Do not ask about military unit (may ask about duration and general role if relevant)
- [ ] Do not ask about political views or voting patterns
- [ ] Do not ask about health conditions unrelated to job duties
- [ ] Provide reasonable accommodations for candidates with disabilities
- [ ] Ask all candidates the same core questions
- [ ] Document interview scores and decision rationale

## Key Amendments

| Year | Amendment | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 1995 | Added sexual orientation | Extended protection to LGBTQ+ employees |
| 1998 | Added parental status | Protected parents and non-parents equally |
| 2010 | Added appearance | Prohibited discrimination based on physical appearance |
| 2014 | Strengthened reserve duty protections | Increased penalties for miluim discrimination |
| 2017 | Added place of residence | Prevented geographic discrimination |

## Related Laws

- **Hours of Work and Rest Law 1951** (chok sha'ot avoda u'menucha): Working hours, overtime, rest days
- **Equal Pay Law 1996** (chok shchar shave le'ovedet u'le'oved): Equal pay for equal work regardless of gender
- **Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law 1998** (chok shivyon zchuyot le'anashim im mugbaluyot): Workplace accommodations and accessibility
- **Prevention of Sexual Harassment Law 1998** (chok le'mni'at hat'radah minit): Employer obligations to prevent and address harassment
- **Employment of Women Law 1954** (chok avodot nashim): Maternity leave, protection from dismissal during pregnancy
