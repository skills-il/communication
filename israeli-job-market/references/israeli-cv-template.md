# Israeli CV Template

## Standard Israeli CV Format

This template follows Israeli hiring conventions. Customize based on whether you are targeting
Israeli companies (Hebrew) or multinational/tech companies (English).

## Key Differences: Israeli vs. US CV

| Element | Israel | United States |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Photo | Common and expected | Not included |
| Personal details | Date of birth, marital status accepted | Not included |
| Military service | Expected section | Not applicable |
| Length | 1-2 pages | 1 page (junior), 2 pages (senior) |
| Language | Hebrew or English depending on company | English only |
| References | "Available upon request" or included | "Available upon request" |

## Tips for Specific Sectors

### Tech/Startup
- English CV preferred
- Emphasize GitHub, open source contributions
- List specific technologies prominently
- Military tech unit experience is highly valued
- Include hackathon or side project experience

### Finance/Banking
- Hebrew CV often preferred
- Formal tone, conservative formatting
- CPA/CFA certifications prominent
- Emphasize regulatory knowledge (ISA, Bank of Israel)

### Government/Public Sector
- Hebrew CV required
- Formal structure
- Include teudat zehut (ID) number if requested
- Military service section important
- Emphasize relevant degrees from recognized institutions

### Healthcare
- Hebrew CV for Israeli institutions
- Include medical license number
- Residency and fellowship details
- Research publications if applicable
- Language skills important (Hebrew, English, Arabic, Russian)
